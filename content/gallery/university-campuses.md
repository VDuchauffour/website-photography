London's universities built aggressively in concrete during the 1960s and 1970s. The expansion of higher education demanded new lecture theatres, libraries, and student housing at speed and scale — and brutalism delivered.

The results range from the celebrated (the Barbican, the National Theatre nearby) to the controversial (the Institute of Education tower, various UCL buildings). This series documents the concrete campus architecture of Bloomsbury and the South Bank — buildings that students and staff either love fiercely or would happily see demolished.
