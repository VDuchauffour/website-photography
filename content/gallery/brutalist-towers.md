The panel housing estates of Prague's outer districts represent one of the largest brutalist building programmes in Central Europe. Between 1960 and 1990, hundreds of thousands of prefabricated concrete apartments were erected across the city's southern and eastern edges.

This series documents the towers of Jižní Město — a district of nearly 100,000 residents living in a landscape of repeating concrete forms. Shot across multiple visits in different weather conditions to capture how light and atmosphere transform these monolithic structures.
