Tbilisi's Soviet-era housing is among the most dramatic in the former USSR. Built on steep hillsides, the apartment blocks stack and cascade in ways their Moscow-designed plans never intended. Residents added balconies, extensions, and entire rooms, creating an organic layer of human adaptation over rigid Soviet geometry.

The Nutsubidze Plateau district is the crown jewel — interconnected towers with sky bridges and a sense of vertical density that feels almost science-fictional.
