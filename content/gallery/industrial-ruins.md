Ostrava was the steel heart of Czechoslovakia. The Vítkovice Iron Works operated for over 180 years before closing in 1998. What remains is one of Europe's most impressive industrial ruins — blast furnaces, gasometers, cooling towers, and kilometres of pipework slowly being reclaimed by nature and reimagined as cultural spaces.

The scale is overwhelming. Standing inside the blast furnace building, surrounded by concrete and steel structures that dwarf anything in residential architecture, you understand why brutalism borrowed so heavily from industrial aesthetics.
