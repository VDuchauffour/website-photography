After the devastating 1963 earthquake, Skopje was rebuilt as a showcase of modernist and brutalist civic architecture. Kenzo Tange designed the master plan, but it was local architects like Janko Konstantinov who created the city's most striking buildings.

The Main Post Office and Telecommunications Centre — with its clustered cylindrical towers and elevated walkways — is one of the most photogenic brutalist buildings anywhere. These structures represented optimism, modernity, and national pride expressed in raw concrete.
