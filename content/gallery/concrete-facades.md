Switzerland's relationship with béton brut is deeper than most realise. The country that gave the world Le Corbusier continued to build in raw concrete long after other nations moved on. Zürich's university campuses and civic buildings from the 1960s–1980s offer some of the finest board-marked concrete surfaces in Europe.

This series focuses purely on surface — the textures, patterns, and weathering of exposed concrete facades across Zürich's institutional buildings.
