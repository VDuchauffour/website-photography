Multi-storey car parks are brutalism's most honest buildings. No pretence of beauty, no decorative ambition — just structure solving a problem. Concrete ramps, exposed services, fluorescent light fighting with daylight through ventilation slots.

And yet there's an accidental beauty to these spaces. The spiral ramps create dramatic perspectives. The repeating structural bays produce hypnotic rhythms. When empty, they become cathedrals of raw concrete.
